Guide 05

All-in-one manager capability map

First Touch is designed as one operating system for management execution: from thinking and planning to real actions and reporting.

On this page

Cognitive work Content and communication Meetings and coordination Action automation Reporting and governance

1) Cognitive work and decision support

  • Ask strategic questions from global context across company systems.
  • Get decision briefs with risks, tradeoffs, and recommended next actions.
  • Compare scenarios and build decision-ready summaries for leadership.

2) Content, writing, and technical creation

  • Draft emails, strategy docs, updates, and customer-facing materials.
  • Create technical task specs, implementation notes, and code scaffolding.
  • Align communication style with brand and executive voice.

3) Meetings and planning loop

  • Transcribe meetings and extract structured action items.
  • Create follow-ups, assign owners, and schedule deadlines.
  • Sync planning outputs with calendar and task workflows.

4) Action primitives and workflow execution

Calendar actionsCreate/update events and focus blocks.
Task orchestrationCreate, route, update, and close work items.
ApprovalsRun policy-based approve/reject checkpoints.
Cross-system syncMove context between CRM, support, docs, and ops.

5) Reporting, cadence, and governance

  • Generate weekly and monthly operational reporting automatically.
  • Track KPI deltas, blockers, and execution health by lane.
  • Maintain auditable action history for compliance and reviews.

The result is one assistant layer that reduces context switching, keeps execution quality high, and scales management bandwidth without linear headcount increase.
