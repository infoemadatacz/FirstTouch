Compliance and governance by design

Governance is not an add-on. In First Touch, it is built into routing, permissions, approvals, and logs from day one.

On this page

Control model

First Touch uses policy thresholds to decide if an action is autonomous, approval-required, or blocked. This keeps operational speed high without losing control.

  • Policy thresholds by workflow lane and risk class.
  • Escalation paths mapped to specific business owners.
  • Safe mode fallback for partial outages or uncertain context.

Role and permission logic

Each action is validated against role scope before execution. This allows one assistant layer to serve multiple departments without permission leakage.

RBACAction rights by role, team, and environment.
Approval tiersThresholds for finance, legal, and operational actions.
Context boundariesAccess only to fields needed for current task.
Emergency stopImmediate autonomy freeze with human override.

Audit architecture

Every workflow step is logged as an event sequence. Teams can explain why an action happened, who approved it, and which data were used.

  • Traceable timeline from intake to resolution.
  • Policy decision logs for every autonomy boundary.
  • Exportable records for compliance and internal review.

Regulated use scenarios

For high-accountability sectors, First Touch supports a human-in-the-loop default with selective autonomy in low-risk operations. This is usually the fastest path to measurable outcomes while preserving legal and governance standards.
