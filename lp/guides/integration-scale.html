Scale model: integration-first, lane by lane

First Touch is designed to grow with your business system architecture, not against it. Start narrow, expand predictably, keep control.

Lane-based rollout strategy

Scale is achieved by proving one constrained workflow lane first, then replicating that operating model into adjacent lanes.

  • Lane 1: highest-friction operational bottleneck.
  • Lane 2-3: adjacent workflows sharing data or ownership.
  • Lane 4+: cross-department orchestration with common governance.

Composable architecture principles

API-first connectorsIntegrate with current systems instead of replacing them.
Model-agnostic coreSwitch or upgrade models without platform rebuild.
Policy portabilityReuse governance rules across new lanes.
Observability layerCompare baseline vs live deltas at workflow level.

Economics of expansion

The cost profile improves as connector reuse increases. New lanes become faster and cheaper to launch when orchestration primitives are shared.

  • Reduced implementation time after first lane.
  • Lower marginal integration cost per additional workflow.
  • Higher operational leverage without linear headcount growth.

Typical expansion roadmap

Weeks 1-8 focus on pilot lane. Weeks 9-16 add adjacent lanes. After that, governance and data models are stable enough for broader rollouts.
