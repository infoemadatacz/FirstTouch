What autonomous means in First Touch

Autonomous does not mean uncontrolled. It means that repetitive and low-risk tasks run automatically under pre-approved rules, while high-risk actions route to humans.

  • Low-risk: routing, categorization, enrichment, status updates, reminders.
  • Medium-risk: proposed approvals with confirmation checkpoints.
  • High-risk: financial, legal, HR-sensitive actions with mandatory human approval.

These capabilities mirror patterns already used in your operational stack, including chat-command execution, calendar actions, and workflow scheduling documented in your current assistant systems.

Human handoff model

Every autonomous workflow has explicit thresholds. When confidence, policy, or risk level crosses a boundary, First Touch pauses and asks for approval with all required context attached.

  • Clear reason for escalation.
  • Pre-filled recommendation and expected impact.
  • One-click approve/reject path with full audit event.

90-day launch path

Start with one lane where operational drag is visible. Measure cycle time, routing latency, and rework from week 1. Expand only when baseline deltas are stable.
