What changes for the group

Three problems that holding groups hit with every AI tool they try.

Generic AI tools force a choice: either give all subsidiaries access to each other's data, or manage dozens of separate tools with no group visibility. FirstTouch is built specifically for multi-entity structures — you don't have to choose.

HQ gets the group view. Subsidiaries keep their privacy.

Your group CEO needs to know how the portfolio is performing — revenue, headcount, key risks, operational status — without waiting for each MD to compile a report. At the same time, your subsidiary MDs reasonably expect that their data isn't visible to sister companies.

FirstTouch delivers both. The group layer shows consolidated metrics and strategic summaries. Each subsidiary's AI environment is fully isolated — its documents, its conversations, its data cannot be accessed by any other entity in the group, including HQ staff who don't have explicit access rights.

How we deploy it

Live across your group in three steps.

No long IT project. No separate procurement for each subsidiary. We work at group level from day one — which means your operating companies get up and running faster than if each tried to do it independently.

01

We map your group structure.

Before we configure anything, we sit down with whoever manages the group — typically the Group CEO or CFO — and map out who reports to whom, which data belongs to which entity, and what the group level actually needs to see. This conversation takes two hours and determines the entire access control architecture. Most clients are surprised by how much clarity this creates internally, even before the technology is involved.

02

We deploy isolated environments per company.

Each operating company gets its own AI environment — separate knowledge base, separate conversation history, separate access controls. A logistics subsidiary cannot query the manufacturing subsidiary's data, and vice versa. Simultaneously, we configure the group layer: a consolidated view that surfaces portfolio-wide metrics without exposing the underlying data from any individual entity. All of this runs on infrastructure you control — on-premise or a private cloud managed by your group IT function.

03

Group dashboard and subsidiary AI, live in 10 days.

By day 10, your group level has a working dashboard that answers strategic questions about the portfolio. Each subsidiary MD has a working AI assistant that knows their business. We run a 30-day review with the group leadership team: what's being used, what time is being saved, what the ROI looks like. At the end of 30 days, you have concrete data — not a promise.
