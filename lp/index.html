What does the setup fee actually cover?

Infrastructure provisioning (VPS or your environment), context architecture (loading your documents, processes, and knowledge base), integration connections (calendar, email, M365 or Google Workspace), governance configuration (autonomy thresholds, RBAC, audit trail), and a live handover session. The setup fee is one-time — it never recurs.

Are model API costs (Claude, GPT-4) included?

Model costs are passed through at cost — you pay exactly what the provider charges, with zero markup. For most deployments running a mix of local (Ollama) and cloud models, this adds €50–€200/month depending on usage. Compute-heavy operations are routed to local models by default to keep cloud spend low.

Can I start on Starter and upgrade to Premium later?

Yes. Starter is a ROI-validation entry point — one workflow lane for 30–60 days gives you concrete data on time saved. Upgrading to Premium is a scoping call plus a delta setup fee (not the full €7,500 again), prorated based on infrastructure already in place.

Is there a minimum contract term?

Monthly billing, with a 90-day recommended minimum to see full ROI. The system takes ~30 days to build effective context on your business — after which value compounds. The Pilot is a flat 14-day engagement — no monthly commitment required. Starter and Premium are month-to-month, with no lock-in, but the value compounds strongest after 90 days.

What exactly is the Pilot — and how does the €500 credit work?

The Pilot is a 14-day live engagement: we deploy one real workflow inside your business (e.g. meeting transcription + follow-up, or a reporting automation), you see actual output, and we measure time saved together. The flat €500 fee covers setup and daily support during the pilot. If you then move to Starter or Premium, the full €500 is credited against your Month 1 invoice — so you pay nothing extra to continue. If you decide not to proceed, you keep everything deployed.

What's the total cost of ownership in year one?

Pilot: €500 flat (fully credited if you continue). Starter: €2,500 setup + €4,800 in monthly fees = €7,300 year-one total, plus €50–€200/mo in pass-through model API costs. Premium: €7,500 setup + €15,000 in monthly fees = €22,500. Compare to a human Chief of Staff at €60,000–€90,000/year in salary alone — before benefits or a 3–6 month ramp. The math compounds quickly after month two.

How is our data handled — GDPR and EU compliance?

All data remains within your infrastructure — VPS, private cloud, or your own servers. We do not store client data on our systems. A GDPR-compliant Data Processing Agreement (DPA) is included in every plan. Enterprise customers can opt for EU-resident infrastructure with full data residency documentation. We operate under EU law and accommodate DSGVO / GDPR requirements for any EU jurisdiction.

What happens when I cancel — do I lose my data?

Cancel anytime with 30 days' notice on Starter or Premium. Your infrastructure, all context data, integrations, and configurations remain fully yours. We provide a complete offboarding package: documentation, credential handover, and export of all agent memory and workflows. No exit fees, no data held hostage.

FAQ

Direct answers for enterprise buyers and operators.

Is this just a ChatGPT wrapper?

No. First Touch is a model-agnostic execution layer deployed on your own infrastructure — not a chat interface pointed at a single API. It routes tasks to the best model (Claude, GPT-4, local Ollama) based on sensitivity and complexity, holds persistent business context across sessions, and takes real autonomous actions: assigning tasks, updating calendars, triggering workflows. Every action is logged with a full audit trail. A ChatGPT wrapper does none of that.

Can it really handle coding, writing, planning, and reporting in one place?

Yes — because all of those tasks share the same underlying need: persistent business context and best-model routing. The assistant drafts strategy documents using your live company context, supports technical planning and code scaffolding, transcribes meetings and extracts action items, and auto-generates weekly leadership reports in your format. The key advantage is that context built during a planning session is immediately available when generating the follow-up report — nothing resets between tasks.

Do we need to replace CRM, ERP, calendar, or support systems?

No — and this is by design. First Touch sits above your current stack as an orchestration layer, connecting to your existing tools through integrations rather than replacing them. Your CRM stays your CRM. Your calendar stays your calendar. The assistant reads from and writes to those systems on your behalf, meaning your team keeps using the tools they already know while the coordination overhead disappears.

How is compliance handled for autonomous actions?

Through four layered controls. First, a governance threshold matrix defines exactly which action types the assistant can take autonomously vs. which require human approval. Second, role-based access control (RBAC) ensures users and the assistant only operate within their permitted scope. Third, every AI call and resulting action is logged in a tamper-evident audit trail — timestamped, attributed, and exportable. Fourth, sensitive data operations can be routed exclusively to local models (Ollama on your VPS), so no data ever leaves your infrastructure. GDPR-compliant DPA documentation is included from day one.

Who should own this internally?

The most effective ownership model we've seen: COO or Head of Operations as the primary business owner (defines workflows, governs autonomy thresholds, monitors KPIs), with IT/Security as the technical sign-off authority (reviews architecture, manages VPS access, approves integration scopes), and CEO or C-suite as the executive sponsor (allocates pilot budget, unblocks integration access). This three-role structure covers the full buying committee and ensures the system gets the access it needs to deliver measurable results.

What is the first step before a pilot?

A 15-minute workflow assessment call — we identify the one high-friction lane with the clearest business cost (usually meeting overhead, report generation, or intake routing), define three baseline KPIs to measure before and after, and map your governance thresholds. From that call, you get a written implementation scope and a go/no-go decision framework so your team can evaluate fit without a long procurement process.

How long does a full deployment take?

For a Starter pilot focused on one workflow lane — typically 8–14 days from contract signature to first live test. This covers VPS provisioning, context upload, integration connections, and governance configuration. Premium deployments with 3 workflow lanes and full context architecture run 3–5 weeks. The fastest path is having your IT team grant VPS access within the first 48 hours — that single dependency drives most of the variance in timelines.

What if we already use Microsoft 365 or Google Workspace?

Both are supported as first-class integrations. First Touch connects to your calendar (Google Calendar or Outlook), reads from and writes to documents (Google Docs or SharePoint), and processes meeting recordings from Teams or Meet. You don't migrate or change tools — the assistant surfaces inside your existing workflow via Telegram while operating on data that lives in your M365 or GWS environment. All data access is scoped, logged, and reversible.

How does the cost compare to hiring a Chief of Staff?

A senior Chief of Staff in Central Europe costs €60,000–€90,000 per year in salary alone, plus benefits, onboarding time (typically 3–6 months before full effectiveness), and the natural ceiling of one person's bandwidth. First Touch at the Premium tier is €15,000/year all-in — and it operates 24/7, handles multiple parallel workflows, never resets context, and delivers the first measurable delta within 30 days. The right framing isn't replacement: it's giving your existing leadership team the leverage of a CoS without the hiring overhead or the single-point-of-failure risk.
