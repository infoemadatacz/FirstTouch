Why UK Law Firms Can't Use ChatGPT for Client Work — And What to Use Instead

Across the UK, solicitors and partners are quietly using ChatGPT to draft letters, summarise documents, and research case law. Some are doing it with client data included. Most don't realise the professional and regulatory exposure they're creating — until something goes wrong.

The Problem No One Talks About in Briefings

Let's be direct: ChatGPT and most AI tools built on public cloud infrastructure are not appropriate for handling client matter data at UK law firms. This isn't a technicality. It's a compliance position with real consequences.

When you or a fee earner at your firm types a client's name, matter details, commercial situation, or personal circumstances into ChatGPT, that data is transmitted to and processed by OpenAI's servers. It leaves your environment. You no longer control it.

That creates three distinct exposure areas simultaneously:

SRA Risk

SRA Principles require firms to protect client confidentiality. Using unvetted third-party tools with client data without informed consent and documented due diligence is a potential conduct breach.

UK GDPR Risk

Client personal data processed outside your controlled environment requires a lawful basis, a Data Processing Agreement with the processor, and compliance with data minimisation and purpose limitation obligations.

PI Exposure

If a client suffers loss because their confidential information was mishandled through an AI tool — and your firm cannot demonstrate adequate due diligence — your professional indemnity cover may not respond.

None of this means AI cannot help law firms. It means the wrong AI tools create serious risk, and most firms haven't drawn the line clearly enough.

What the SRA Actually Says

The SRA has published specific guidance on AI use in legal practice. The key points are not ambiguous:

  • Firms must understand how AI tools process and store data before using them with client information
  • Adequate supervision of AI-assisted work is a professional requirement — not optional
  • Client confidentiality obligations apply to AI tool use as much as any other context
  • Claiming you were unaware of how a tool handled data is not a mitigating factor

This puts the onus firmly on the firm — not the tool provider — to ensure appropriate use. A "we didn't know" response after a confidentiality breach involving an AI tool is not a defence under SRA standards.

The SRA's position is clear: firms are responsible for understanding every tool that handles client information. Ignorance is not a defence.

Why "Enterprise ChatGPT" Doesn't Fix This

Some firms assume that upgrading to a paid enterprise version of a public AI tool resolves the compliance concern. In most cases, it doesn't — or it resolves only part of it.

Enterprise arrangements typically offer:

  • A promise not to use your data for model training
  • A Data Processing Agreement
  • Some regional data residency options

What they typically don't offer:

  • Data processed entirely within your own infrastructure
  • Matter-level access controls aligned with your conflict-checking system
  • A full audit log that your COLP can review
  • Guaranteed UK data residency in a specific certified environment

For most UK law firms' client data, "better than free ChatGPT" is not the right standard. The standard is: does this deployment meet our professional conduct obligations and UK GDPR requirements? For public cloud tools, the honest answer is: often not fully.

What Compliant AI for a Law Firm Actually Looks Like

The architecture that makes AI safe for legal work is not complicated, but it does require deliberate setup:

Requirement Public AI Tools On-Premise AI
Data stays in your environment No — processed externally Yes — runs on your infrastructure
Matter-level access controls Not available Built in from day one
Full audit trail Limited or none Every query logged and attributable
No model training on your data Enterprise only, with caveats Guaranteed — your data is not shared
UK GDPR compliance Requires DPA review; residency varies Fully controlled — you are the data controller
SRA supervisory audit support Cannot evidence oversight Complete logs for COLP review

On-premise AI means the AI system runs on infrastructure you control — either in your building or in a dedicated private environment. Client data is processed locally. Nothing leaves. You maintain full visibility and control.

What This Enables in Practice

Once your AI deployment is genuinely compliant, the productivity gains in legal work are significant and immediate:

Document search. Fee earners query your entire document library in natural language. Finding the relevant precedent, the applicable clause in a past agreement, or the regulatory reference takes seconds, not 20 minutes.

Matter summarisation. Before a client call or a supervision meeting, the AI pulls together the key facts, timeline, and open issues from the matter file. No more re-reading the file from the beginning.

Standard correspondence drafting. Engagement letters, client update emails, file notes, instructions to counsel — first drafts produced in seconds. Your lawyers review, refine, and approve rather than writing from scratch.

Deadline and compliance monitoring. Limitation periods, regulatory filing dates, renewal deadlines — surfaced automatically before they become problems.

The pattern across all of these is the same: work that currently takes 20–30 minutes per instance is done in 2–3 minutes. For a firm of 20 fee earners, that is a substantial amount of recovered time every week.
