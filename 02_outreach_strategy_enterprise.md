# First Touch
## Enterprise Outreach Strategy (Unified with EMA AI Personal Brand)

Last update: 2026-02-24

## 1. Core Constraint

Your LinkedIn must read as one strategic story:
- **Primary identity:** Founder/operator building EMA AI.
- **Operational wedge:** First Touch orchestration.
- **Strategic horizon:** AI team leader end-game in regulated finance.

If this order is reversed, the profile feels fragmented.

## 2. Identity Split (What to Lead With)

Default external positioning:
- Lead as `CEO/Founder, EMA AI`.
- Sell business outcomes in EMA context.
- Use First Touch as the mechanism, not the headline identity.

Rule:
- In 80% of conversations, First Touch appears only after problem relevance is established.

## 3. Message Architecture (Single Narrative)

### Master line

"I build EMA AI for financial services in CEE.  
First Touch is the orchestration layer we use to turn AI capability into operational throughput."

### Message house

1. Problem:
- Leadership teams lose throughput in fragmented intake and approvals.

2. Mechanism:
- First Touch applies governed intake-to-execution orchestration.

3. Proof:
- EMA stack use-cases and field implementation patterns.

4. Direction:
- This is a practical step toward AI team-leader operations.

## 4. Content Portfolio (Anti-Schizo Allocation)

Align with your existing Personal-Brand-Kluz mix:
- Business: 35%
- AI: 30%
- Lifestyle: 20%
- Mindset: 15%

Brand exposure ratio:
- EMA Core: 70-80%
- Founder POV: 15-20%
- First Touch Signal: 5-10%

Map to narrative lanes:
- Business lane -> EMA partnerships, distribution, execution decisions.
- AI lane -> First Touch architecture, orchestration design, governance.
- Lifestyle lane -> founder operating system, routines behind execution.
- Mindset lane -> decision principles, trade-offs, leadership framing.

Rule:
- Every First Touch post must reference EMA context or end-game direction.

## 5. Targeting and Account Strategy

### Tier A
- Regulated enterprises and major operators in CEE.
- CEO/COO + Ops + IT/Security stakeholders.

### Tier B
- Scaleups with visible operational bottlenecks.

### Tier C
- Fast-mover lighthouse accounts for short-cycle proof cases.

## 6. Outreach Stack

You run four layers together:

1. Authority posts:
- 3-4 high-signal posts weekly.

2. Warm prospecting:
- Connect after visible engagement or clear fit signals.

3. Relationship DMs:
- No pitch first.
- Move from relevance -> insight -> diagnostic question.

4. Working-call conversion:
- Invite only after intent signal.
- Position as a workflow mapping session, not a sales intro.

## 7. Two-Motion Outreach Logic

### Motion A (Primary, EMA-led)
- Narrative: "We solve X operational bottleneck in EMA AI context."
- Offer: workflow map + diagnostic sprint.
- Use for most enterprise prospects.

### Motion B (Secondary, First Touch-labeled)
- Narrative: "First Touch is the orchestration framework behind this result."
- Offer: focused working session on one workflow.
- Use only after reply, pain signal, or repeat engagement.

## 8. Weekly Execution Rhythm

Monday:
- Publish one business/strategy lane post.
- Send 15-20 Tier A connection requests.

Tuesday:
- Comment sprint on target operators.
- Follow-up DM wave 1.

Wednesday:
- Publish one AI/orchestration lane post.
- Send 15-20 connection requests.

Thursday:
- DM wave 2 (value + diagnostic).

Friday:
- Publish one proof/implementation post.
- Convert qualified threads to working-call invites.

Weekend:
- Optional founder memo (mindset/lifestyle lane).

## 9. Call Conversion Rules

Offer a call only if one is true:
- They describe an active bottleneck.
- They ask for implementation detail.
- They engage repeatedly and confirm relevance.

Default timing:
- Invite between Day 7 and Day 14 after connection acceptance.

Call format:
- 25-minute working session.
- One workflow map.
- First autonomy threshold proposal.

Additional guardrail:
- If they do not know EMA context yet, do not use "future founder of First Touch" framing.
- Keep first call invitation under EMA operating-improvement narrative.

## 10. Qualification Before Call

Qualify in DM:
- Is this priority active this quarter?
- Is there an owner for the workflow?
- Is a constrained pilot acceptable?

Disqualify when:
- Pure curiosity with no mandate.
- No operational ownership.
- No willingness to run measured pilot scope.

## 11. KPI Control

Top:
- Connection acceptance by persona.
- Engagement from target accounts.

Middle:
- Reply rate by DM step.
- Diagnostic-question response rate.

Bottom:
- Calls booked.
- Show rate.
- Pilot proposal rate.

Brand split controls:
- % of posts by brand track.
- % of booked calls sourced from EMA-led motion.
- % of threads where First Touch is introduced too early.

## 12. Non-Negotiable Style Rules

- English only.
- No clickbait hooks.
- No generic AI hype language.
- No side-hustle framing.
- Show decision logic and trade-offs.
- Stay operator-level and evidence-led.
