# First Touch
## Brand Architecture: EMA AI x First Touch x Personal Brand

Last update: 2026-02-24

## 1. Narrative Stack (Top to Bottom)

1. **Who you are**
- Founder/operator building EMA AI in CEE financial services.

2. **What system you are building**
- EMA AI as integrated product stack (Talent, Market Research, Recommendation, Certification direction).

3. **How it operates in practice**
- First Touch as the orchestration mechanism for intake-to-execution workflow control.

4. **Where it goes**
- Toward AI team-leader capability under governance constraints.

## 2. Brand Portfolio Allocation (Hard Split)

Use this as a strict weekly ratio:

- `EMA Core`: 70-80%
- `Founder Operator POV`: 15-20%
- `First Touch Signal`: 5-10%

Interpretation:
- EMA AI is the main brand and primary credibility engine.
- Founder POV supports trust and decision authority.
- First Touch appears as a focused operating framework, not as a parallel identity.

## 3. One-Sentence Positioning

"I build EMA AI for regulated financial operations.  
First Touch is the orchestration layer we use to turn AI capability into measurable operational throughput."

## 4. Identity Separation Rules

### Public identity (always primary)
- CEO/founder building EMA AI.

### Secondary identity (contextual)
- Operator designing workflow orchestration systems.

### Future venture signal (limited and explicit)
- "First Touch" is introduced as a focused initiative or framework inside current operator work.

Do not present yourself as:
- "Running two separate companies now."
- "Leaving EMA to build First Touch."

## 5. Message House

### Strategic claim
- AI value comes from workflow orchestration, not model novelty.

### Supporting claims
- Data + process + governance beat prompt optimization.
- Narrow pilot scope outperforms platform-wide AI ambition.
- Human-in-the-loop is a speed enabler in regulated contexts.

### Proof types
- Workflow before/after maps.
- Measured pilot KPIs.
- Field insights from EMA use-cases.

## 6. Channel Placement (Where Each Identity Lives)

- `LinkedIn headline/about`: EMA-first identity only.
- `Most posts`: EMA strategy, product execution, regulated AI operations.
- `Selected posts`: First Touch mechanics tied to EMA operating reality.
- `DM outreach`: Start EMA-first; introduce First Touch only after relevance signal.

## 7. Content Guardrails

Always do:
- Anchor posts in current operator work.
- Show trade-offs and decision logic.
- Keep language practical, short, and evidence-led.

Never do:
- "AI side hustle" framing.
- Generic AI evangelism.
- Product announcements without operational context.
- Detached thought-leadership with no implementation path.

## 8. Post-Level Consistency Check

Before publishing, verify:

1. Does this post clearly connect to EMA operator reality?
2. Is the brand track clear (`EMA Core`, `Founder POV`, or `First Touch Signal`)?
3. If First Touch is mentioned, is it presented as EMA operating layer or structured initiative?
4. Is the post still strengthening EMA credibility first?
5. Does it include implementation logic, not just opinion?
6. Would a target COO/RevOps lead see a clear business implication?

If any answer is "no," rewrite before posting.

## 9. DM-Level Consistency Check

Before sending call invite:

1. Did the conversation start in EMA context?
2. Was First Touch introduced only as mechanism/framework?
3. Is the offer framed as workflow outcome, not side-project demo?

If not, reset message sequence before asking for call.

## 10. Offer Ladder Alignment

Public content should ladder into one consistent commercial path:

1. Workflow bottleneck map (content CTA).
2. 25-minute working session (DM CTA).
3. Diagnostic sprint.
4. Constrained pilot.
5. Scale program.

No detached offers outside this ladder.
